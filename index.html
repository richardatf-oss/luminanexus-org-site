Weaving Torah, creativity, and technology into a single field of light.

LuminaNexus.org is a small nonprofit with a big kavanah: to build tools, stories, and sanctuaries that help people remember that reality is alive, connected, and full of meaning.

Rooted in Kingman, AZ · Facing Jerusalem

The Quantum Etz Chaim

One Tree at the center, with each project as an orbiting light: apps, sefarim, stories, and sanctuaries arising from a single living Field.

Our mission

To create bridges between sacred text, modern tools, and human hearts— so that learning feels like standing under a living Tree of Light.

Guard the light

Preserve and share Torah-rooted wisdom in ways that honor tradition while speaking in a modern voice.

Build new vessels

Design apps, stories, and experiences that let people encounter holiness through beauty, play, and curiosity.

Heal through connection

Use technology as a nexus—linking people, projects, and places into one field of mutual support and repair.

Current & emerging projects

A glimpse of the constellation of work orbiting the Quantum Etz Chaim. Some projects are live, some in active development.

The Celestial Library of the 231 Gates

A long-form exploration of the Quantum Etz Chaim and the 231 Gates as living pathways of consciousness and field-theory-meets-Kabbalah.

Status: In development

Ivrit HaOr

A sacred Hebrew learning experience integrating Biblical and modern Hebrew, gematria, Kabbalah, and the hidden letter אלף עולם, with audio, art, and meditation.

Status: Prototype phase

ChavrutaGPT

An AI-assisted chavruta companion to help learners explore sources and ask better questions while staying rooted in tradition.

Status: R&D

Rider of the Forgotten Star

A Western-planetary romance series inspired by classic pulp adventure, dedicated to family, faith, and quiet heroism.

Status: Manuscript in progress

How you can help

LuminaNexus is small, intentional, and relational. We grow through real people offering what they love to do.

Support financially

Offer your skills

Designers, developers, editors, educators, cantors, rabbis, musicians, and makers are all welcome in this field of work.

Spread the word

Share projects with friends, communities, and institutions that might resonate with this blend of Torah and tech.
