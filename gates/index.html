The 231 Gates

Between every two letters of the Aleph-Bet there is a Gate—a path of meaning, movement, and possible worlds. This page is a first, simple map: a place to play with a few sample Gates and sketch how a full star-map might grow.

One Field, many Gates

In classic Kabbalah, the 231 Gates are the pairings of the twenty-two letters. Each pairing—like אב, אג, or את—can be read as a tiny bridge between sefirot, Trees, and ideas.

Under the Quantum Etz Chaim, each Gate can connect:

  • Two letters and their directions in the Field,
  • Two sefirot or modes of the unified Field,
  • Two Trees or Sefer-projects (like Etz HaReishit and Etz HaEven),
  • Or two human questions that are secretly the same conversation.

This first version keeps things deliberately simple: just a small sample of Gates, each with a short note about how it might link letters, sefirot, and Trees. Over time, this can grow into an interactive star-field you can walk through.

Roadmap: from grid to star-map

  1. Stage 1 — This page. A small grid of Gates with hand-written notes, wired to a simple detail panel.
  2. Stage 2 — Full grid of 231 Gates. Every pairing appears as a clickable node, with tags for letters, sefirot, and Trees.
  3. Stage 3 — Star-field view. Gates plotted as a constellation, where you can wander paths or filter by sefirah, Tree, or project.
  4. Stage 4 — Sources under each Gate. Each Gate links into the Library and Sefaria with relevant texts, commentaries, and project notes.
